# What is High Numeracy?

It's likely better to ask: what do we mean when we refer to someone as smart? For many, smart is a generalization for a host of characterstics that tend to point at: high numeracy. It includes things like: innate or achieved abilities in mathematics, science literacy, trivial recall, reading comprehension and critical thinking, or even devoid any particular subject matter and rather, a robust vernacular and rhetorician skillset.

# Is High Numeracy Better?

For many, achieving the status of *smart* is a goal. But, it really just means developing numeracy abilities. Numeracy comes from statitics, where it referred to people with an ability to understand, manipluate, and reason using numbers. Numeracy has since out growth that parochial framing, and expanded to mean individuals with deep reasoning abilities in other fields where technical and analytic thinking is necessary.

The question of better is besides the point ultimately, because it depends on individual needs. There are plenty of people who are functionally literate within their own field without requiring high numeracy in said field. It's important for pushing the envelope forward, but not necessary by any means.

The bigger issue with high numeracy I'd like to explore is how high numeracy bleeds into a covert dunning kruger (DK) effect. Individuating covert DK from DK, covert DK focuses on how individuals who hold high numeracy in one or more fields that's warranted (e.g., a genuinely intelligent biologist who is well read in economic thery), but unknowingly blurs technical language across field specific borders without doing the groundwork that warrants it, or stipulating specifically where on discipline ends, and the next starts.

The main mechanism behind the covert part is that these individuals believe to be genuinely epistemically sound as opposed to actively laundering credibility. An example might look like a neuroscience who tries to ground a mechanism in a metaphysicsl commitment, but, not realizing the categorical error or providing the necessary engagement with the current literature.
